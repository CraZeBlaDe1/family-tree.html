Note on the Prophet's ﷺ marriages: Each marriage served a specific purpose — protecting widows, honoring tribes, building alliances, abolishing unjust customs, or divine command. Most of his wives were widows or divorcees. He lived monogamously with Khadijah for 25 years. His marriages after her were in his 50s and 60s — not driven by desire but by duty and mercy. All wives after Khadijah are honored as "Mothers of the Believers" — no Muslim may marry them.

On his sons: All three sons died in infancy. Enemies mocked him as "abtar" — cut off with no male heir. Allah responded: "Indeed it is your enemy who is cut off." (Surah Al-Kawthar 108:3). The Prophet's ﷺ lineage continues through Fatimah to this day, and billions call themselves Muslim. His mockers are forgotten.

The living lineage: People who descend from the Prophet ﷺ through Hasan and Husayn are called Sayyids or Sharifs. There are millions worldwide. The King of Jordan and the King of Morocco both claim this lineage. DNA studies have traced a distinct Hashimite genetic marker through these family lines.
